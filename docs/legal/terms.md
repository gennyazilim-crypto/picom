# Picom Terms of Service - Legal Review Draft

> **Draft for qualified legal and product review. Not legal advice, not approved terms, and not ready for public acceptance.** Effective date, operator identity, contact details, governing law, regional terms, and version must be completed before release.

## 1. Service scope

Picom is a desktop community chat application for Windows, Linux, and macOS. It provides accounts, profiles, communities, channels, direct conversations, messages, attachments, reactions, realtime features, voice rooms, and screen sharing. Availability may depend on release channel, region, device permissions, community permissions, and third-party providers.

## 2. Eligibility and accounts

Users must meet the minimum age and legal-capacity requirements selected for each launch region. Users must provide accurate account information, protect account access, and promptly report suspected compromise. Account sharing, automated credential testing, and circumvention of suspension or access controls are prohibited.

The final policy must state whether parental consent or age assurance is required, how organization-managed accounts are handled, and which local consumer rules cannot be waived.

## 3. User-generated content

Users retain whatever rights they lawfully hold in messages, attachments, profile material, community rules, and other submitted content. A narrowly scoped service license is expected to be necessary to host, reproduce, transmit, resize, moderate, back up, and display that content solely to operate and protect Picom. Counsel must finalize its territory, duration, sublicensing, deletion, and backup effects.

Users are responsible for having the rights and permissions needed to submit content. Private-channel labels limit product access; they do not replace user judgment, legal obligations, or endpoint security.

## 4. Communities and moderation

Community owners choose moderators, roles, channels, and local rules within Picom's platform-wide policies. Owners and moderators must use permissions proportionately and may not use private access to harass, exploit, or unlawfully monitor members. Picom may restrict content, features, communities, or accounts for safety, security, legal, or operational reasons.

Reports and moderation appeals are product workflows under development. The final terms must define notice, evidence, appeal windows, repeat-offender handling, emergency actions, and any legally required statements of reasons.

## 5. Acceptable use

Use is subject to the [Acceptable Use draft](acceptable-use.md) and [Community Guidelines draft](community-guidelines.md). Prohibited conduct includes illegal activity, credible threats, exploitation, harassment, hate, doxxing, impersonation, fraud, spam, malware, unauthorized access, rights infringement, and attempts to bypass permissions, rate limits, or safety controls.

## 6. Voice, screen sharing, and attachments

Users must not record, rebroadcast, or share a live session without all required notices and consent. Screen sharing may expose sensitive desktop information; users must select sources carefully. Uploaded files must be lawful and safe. Picom may validate, scan, quarantine, limit, or remove files, but no security control guarantees that every harmful file will be detected.

## 7. Beta and service changes

During beta, features may be incomplete, interrupted, disabled, or removed. Data migrations, provider outages, client/server compatibility, and staged releases may affect availability. Final production terms require reviewed service commitments, warranty language, support channels, and any mandatory statutory guarantees.

## 8. Suspension and termination

Picom may suspend access when reasonably necessary to prevent harm, comply with law, investigate abuse, or protect service integrity. Users may request account deletion after transferring owned communities. The current production design revokes sessions and starts a review/grace period before controlled anonymization; it does not promise immediate deletion from backups, legal holds, or immutable audit records.

## 9. Intellectual property and feedback

Picom software, branding, and original assets remain with their respective owner. Third-party components remain under their own licenses. The final terms must define the treatment of voluntary product feedback without taking ownership of unrelated user content.

## 10. Disclaimers, liability, and disputes

Warranty disclaimers, liability limits, indemnities, governing law, venue, arbitration/class-action language, and consumer withdrawal rights are intentionally omitted pending qualified regional counsel. No placeholder in this draft should be treated as limiting non-waivable rights.

## 11. International use

Users may access Picom from multiple jurisdictions while infrastructure and providers may process data in other regions. Regional availability, sanctions/export restrictions, government requests, data transfers, consumer protections, and language precedence require launch-by-launch review.

## 12. Contact and changes

The operator's legal name, postal address, privacy contact, support contact, abuse contact, copyright contact, effective date, notice method, archive location, and acceptance evidence must be added before publication.
