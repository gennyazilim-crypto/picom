# Picom Legal and Policy Review Checklist

> Working checklist for qualified counsel and accountable product owners. Completion records decisions; it does not itself establish compliance or constitute legal advice.

## Publication controls

- [ ] Confirm operator legal entity, address, support, privacy, abuse, copyright, and security contacts.
- [ ] Assign owner, approver, version, effective date, review cadence, and archived-policy location.
- [ ] Confirm in-app presentation, acceptance evidence, material-change notice, and language precedence.
- [ ] Replace every draft banner only after written approval.

## Product accuracy

- [ ] Verify supported Windows, Linux, and macOS releases and beta limitations.
- [ ] Verify Supabase and LiveKit entities, roles, regions, contracts, and subprocessors.
- [ ] Verify Electron native permissions, voice/screen-share behavior, uploads, realtime, diagnostics, and release channels against shipped code.
- [ ] Confirm no claim of end-to-end encryption, guaranteed malware detection, guaranteed delivery, or uninterrupted availability.
- [ ] Confirm disabled/post-MVP bots, webhooks, plugins, discovery, analytics, and update features are not presented as generally available.

## Privacy and data governance

- [ ] Map each data category and purpose to lawful bases by region.
- [ ] Complete data inventory, retention schedule, backup/legal-hold rules, and deletion/anonymization procedure.
- [ ] Review authenticated export scope, row limits, delivery, identity verification, and response deadlines.
- [ ] Review 14-day account-deletion grace period, ownership transfer, session revocation, anonymization, and audit preservation.
- [ ] Approve international transfer mechanisms, data residency statements, representatives, DPO, and regulator contacts where required.
- [ ] Review cookies/local storage, device identifiers, diagnostics, crash reporting, abuse signals, and optional analytics consent.
- [ ] Confirm handling of sensitive/special-category data and breach response duties.

## User-generated content and safety

- [ ] Finalize prohibited-content definitions and regional exceptions.
- [ ] Approve community owner/moderator authority and least-privilege responsibilities.
- [ ] Define report intake, acknowledgement, emergency escalation, evidence access, and retention.
- [ ] Define notices, statements of reasons, appeal eligibility/window, reviewer independence, and transparency reporting.
- [ ] Review copyright/trademark notice, counter-notice, repeat-infringer, and agent requirements.
- [ ] Define law-enforcement, preservation, emergency disclosure, and government request procedures.

## Children and age

- [ ] Select minimum age by launch region.
- [ ] Determine parental consent, age assurance, child-safety reporting, and guardian request procedures.
- [ ] Verify communities, voice, screen sharing, discovery, profiles, and direct communication are appropriate for the selected audience.

## Consumer, contract, and liability

- [ ] Select governing law, venue, dispute path, and regional addenda.
- [ ] Review mandatory consumer guarantees, withdrawal/cancellation, accessibility, language, and platform-store obligations.
- [ ] Draft warranty, liability, indemnity, suspension, termination, beta, and service-change clauses without limiting non-waivable rights.
- [ ] Review sanctions, export controls, tax/billing implications, and regional availability even where paid features are not launched.

## Security and operational readiness

- [ ] Confirm incident response, breach notification, vulnerability disclosure, and evidence preservation processes.
- [ ] Review RLS/authorization test evidence, secrets controls, logging redaction, upload quarantine, and desktop IPC boundaries.
- [ ] Confirm support can route privacy, safety, appeal, legal, and security requests without exposing unrelated private data.
