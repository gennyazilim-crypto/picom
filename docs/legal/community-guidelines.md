# Picom Community Guidelines - Legal Review Draft

> **Draft for trust-and-safety, product, and qualified legal review. Not final enforcement policy or legal advice.** Regional exceptions, severity definitions, appeal deadlines, contacts, and enforcement transparency remain to be approved.

## Purpose

Picom should support useful, welcoming desktop communities while protecting privacy, safety, and lawful expression. These guidelines apply across profiles, community names, messages, attachments, reactions, voice rooms, screen sharing, invites, reports, bots/webhooks if later enabled, and attempts to coordinate prohibited conduct elsewhere.

## Be respectful and protect people

- Do not harass, stalk, threaten, shame, or coordinate targeted abuse.
- Do not attack people based on protected characteristics or promote hateful dehumanization.
- Do not encourage self-harm, credible violence, exploitation, or dangerous wrongdoing.
- Do not expose personal or private information without authorization, including private-channel content.
- Do not impersonate a person, organization, moderator, employee, or verified authority.

Counsel and safety specialists must define protected categories, credible-threat escalation, self-harm response, and lawful regional speech constraints.

## Keep Picom safe and authentic

- Do not distribute malware, credential theft, destructive files, scams, spam, or deceptive links.
- Do not probe, bypass, or exploit authentication, permissions, RLS, rate limits, private channels, storage, Electron IPC, realtime, or voice systems.
- Do not manipulate follows, reactions, reports, invites, or discovery through coordinated inauthentic behavior.
- Do not evade restrictions through alternate accounts or communities.

## Respect rights and lawful content boundaries

Users must have rights to content they share. Sexual exploitation, non-consensual intimate material, illegal goods/services, and content facilitating serious unlawful harm are prohibited. Copyright/trademark notice and counter-notice rules, repeat-infringer standards, and local illegality categories require final legal process.

## Community owner and moderator duties

Owners should publish understandable local rules, grant least-privilege roles, protect private channels, respond to reports, and preserve audit integrity. Moderators must use only authorized context, avoid retaliation, document serious actions, and not expose report contents beyond necessary review.

Local rules may be stricter but cannot authorize conduct prohibited by Picom-wide policy or law.

## Reports, enforcement, and appeals

Users should report the most relevant message, profile, attachment, community, or live-session context without adding unrelated private content. Picom or authorized community teams may warn, limit, remove, quarantine, mute, suspend, ban, or refer matters when proportionate.

Report acknowledgement, emergency handling, evidence retention, notice, statements of reasons, appeal eligibility, appeal windows, reviewer independence, and regulator/law-enforcement routing are placeholders pending operational and legal approval. Reports do not guarantee a requested outcome.

## Privacy during live sessions

Do not record, rebroadcast, or expose another person's voice, screen, location, credentials, or private information without required consent. Stop screen sharing before opening sensitive windows. Community rules must not falsely imply that a live room is confidential or end-to-end encrypted.

## Enforcement principles

Decisions should consider severity, intent, context, reach, repetition, user vulnerability, prior actions, and immediate risk. Enforcement should be documented, access-limited, appealable where appropriate, and periodically reviewed for consistency. Picom may act urgently before full review where credible harm or system compromise is suspected.
