# Picom Acceptable Use Policy - Legal Review Draft

> **Draft for qualified legal, security, and trust-and-safety review. Not legal advice and not approved for public enforcement.** Definitions, jurisdictional requirements, sanctions/export language, and appeal process remain open.

## Scope

This draft applies to all Picom accounts, communities, channels, direct conversations, messages, files, reactions, voice rooms, screen shares, invites, integrations, APIs, desktop clients, and attempts to access the service.

## Prohibited conduct

Users must not use Picom to:

1. Conduct or facilitate unlawful activity, credible threats, exploitation, trafficking, or serious physical harm.
2. Harass, stalk, dox, discriminate against, or coordinate abuse of another person.
3. Create, request, distribute, or retain illegal sexual content or non-consensual intimate material.
4. Infringe copyright, trademark, privacy, publicity, confidentiality, or other rights.
5. Impersonate others, misrepresent authority, run scams, manipulate users, or distribute spam.
6. Upload or link malware, credential theft, ransomware, destructive payloads, or files intended to evade scanning/quarantine.
7. Gain unauthorized access; bypass authentication, private-channel permissions, RLS, rate limits, moderation, blocks, bans, or feature controls.
8. Scrape, enumerate, reverse engineer, benchmark abusively, or automate the service beyond documented permission and reasonable limits.
9. Interfere with availability through flooding, realtime event abuse, mass invites, repeated login attempts, upload abuse, or resource exhaustion.
10. Expose secrets, tokens, credentials, personal data, private messages, voice, or screen content without authority and required consent.
11. Evade enforcement through alternate identities, communities, clients, integrations, or coordinated third parties.

## Security research

No safe-harbor promise is made in this draft. A production vulnerability disclosure policy must define authorized targets, testing limits, prohibited data access, rate limits, reporting contacts, confidentiality, and good-faith handling before researchers are invited to test.

## Automated and integration use

Bots, webhooks, plugins, public APIs, and developer keys are not generally available unless explicitly enabled. Future automation must use issued credentials, declared permissions, documented endpoints, and rate limits. It may not imitate users, harvest private content, or execute arbitrary code/file/shell operations on desktop clients.

## Enforcement and reporting

Picom may throttle, quarantine, restrict, suspend, or terminate access and preserve limited evidence where reasonably necessary for security, safety, legal compliance, or investigation. Reporting and appeal interfaces are product foundations; final eligibility, timelines, notices, emergency exceptions, and escalation contacts require approval.

## International restrictions

Users must comply with applicable sanctions, export controls, local law, and legal-capacity requirements. The operator must obtain counsel before claiming availability in a jurisdiction or restricting lawful regional use. Conflicts of law and mandatory consumer/user rights are unresolved in this draft.
